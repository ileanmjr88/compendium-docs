Compendium stands on the work of many open source projects, foundations, and
individuals. The binaries we distribute, the build tools we orchestrate, and
the runtimes we install were all created and maintained by people whose work
deserves recognition, and where applicable, your support.

We link directly to upstream releases. We don't rehost, modify, or repackage
their work unless explicitly noted (vcpkg is the one exception in v0.1, since
it requires assembly of binary plus scripts). Checksums verify integrity end
to end.

If you find Compendium useful, please consider supporting the upstream
projects below. Many are maintained by small teams or individuals whose work
benefits the entire developer community.

## Distribution Partners

### xPack, by Liviu Ionescu

The [xPack project](https://xpack.github.io) provides cross-platform
reproducible binary distributions of the compilers and system utilities
Compendium relies on most heavily: GCC, arm-none-eabi-gcc,
aarch64-none-elf-gcc, riscv-none-elf-gcc, OpenOCD, bison, m4, pkg-config, and
realpath. xPack is maintained primarily by **Liviu Ionescu** and is
load-bearing for a significant portion of Compendium's public registry.

If Compendium is useful to you, please consider sponsoring xPack:

- Website: [xpack.github.io](https://xpack.github.io)
- GitHub: [xpack-dev-tools](https://github.com/xpack-dev-tools)
- **Sponsor:** [ko-fi.com/ilegeul](https://ko-fi.com/ilegeul)

## System Utilities

The following utilities are essential to Compendium's build support. The GNU
tools are maintained by the GNU Project under the stewardship of the Free
Software Foundation. All are distributed via xPack.

- **m4.** Macro processor (GNU)
- **bison.** Parser generator (GNU)
- **realpath.** Path resolution utility (GNU coreutils)
- **pkg-config.** Originated at [freedesktop.org](https://www.freedesktop.org).
  The GNU pkg-config implementation is what Compendium distributes.

**Support the GNU Project:** [fsf.org/donate](https://www.fsf.org/donate)

## A Note on Open Source

Compendium exists because thousands of individuals and organizations have
spent decades building, maintaining, and freely sharing the software the world
runs on. Sometimes that work is funded. Often it isn't. If Compendium helps
you ship faster, consider returning some of that benefit to the projects that
made it possible.
